Teeth Whitening

Patients always come up with queries regarding the color of their teeth and they ask for methods to make their teeth white. We discuss the teeth whitening options and causes of their yellow teeth.

Causes of Teeth Discoloration

External or extrinsic causes:

  • Food and beverages like coffee, cranberry juice, wine, etc.
  • Iron supplements
  • Smoking
  • Poor oral hygiene

Internal or intrinsic causes:

  • Some medicines such as Tetracycline
  • Mouthwash such as Chlorhexidine, when used for more than 4 weeks

Teeth Whitening Options

We offer different teeth whitening options for our patients.

  • We ensure our patients maintain good oral hygiene, avoid certain foods and beverages, and come for routine check-ups.

Different options for teeth whitening

  • Over-the-counter teeth whitening, gel, strips, and trays
  • Teeth whitening systems suggested by dentists
  • In-office teeth whitening by a dental professional or dentist.

Bonding

The tooth is roughened with the help of a conditioning gel and composite resin is applied and hardened with a curing light. After that, the dentist will gloss and shape the resin. Anesthesia is used only if there’s an old restoration or decay present.

Advantages

  • Cost: Bonding is among the most inexpensive procedures.
  • Time: Compared to crowns and veneers, bonding takes less time and completes during a single visit.
  • Invasiveness: Bonding needs minimal tooth removal.

Disadvantages

  • Staining: Bonding starts getting stained over time but crowns and veneers do not.
  • Durability: Crowns and veneers last longer than bonding.

What Are Dental Veneers Made Of

Dental veneers are achieved by means of porcelain or resin material. Mostly, porcelain is considered as it has the precise shining/reflecting properties of natural teeth and also resists staining. It can cover up the underlying tooth color. And resin might stain after a certain period of time.

Indications Of Dental Veneers

  • Severely discolored teeth
  • Crooked/mal-positioned teeth
  • Spaces in between teeth
  • Chipped off/ fractured teeth
  • Worn out teeth
  • Teeth with abnormal shape

Contraindication for Dental Veneers

  • Insufficient teeth structure
  • Teeth with severe gum disease
  • Teeth already having large fillings
  • Excessive spaces in between teeth
  • Severely mal-positioned teeth
  • Patients who have the habit of clenching and night grinding

In the third visit, the dentist will place the veneers against the teeth to check the right fit, shape, and shade. When the right shape and shade are finalized, then they initiate the process of veneer bonding.

What Is Zirconium Varnish?

Zirconium ceramic is created by an extensive cycle of reductive chlorination. At the top of this process, a primary ceramic is attained, zirconia powder which is no longer a metal.
Zirconia is used in white pains and as abrasives. Its state in the monolithic phase is brittle and fragile.

What Is Zirconia Crown?

Zirconia is a strong material that is compatible with the physical body. It is used in many other medical applications like artificial joints. There are no side effects or any fear of the body rejecting zirconia. We supply you with the simplest prosthodontic care by rendering you the best zirconia crown options accessible.

Advantages of Zirconia Crown

  • If you're looking for a robust, long lasting, and visually appealing crown, it can be the best choice.
  • This lasts much longer than the other crowns.
  • They have a beautiful translucent color that blends well with the color of your teeth.

Disadvantages of Zirconia Crown

  • This material has a tough and abrasive quality which causes friction against the basis of the tooth.
